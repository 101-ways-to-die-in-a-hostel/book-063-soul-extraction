# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Soul extraction ritual removes the victim's essence to feed an ancient Balinese hunger deity; body remains alive but empty
**Category:** Supernatural
**Duration:** Hours (ceremony takes 4 hours; soul extraction is the final 10 minutes)
**Witnesses:** Other ceremony participants, but they believe they're experiencing a positive transformation

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Skyler's soul - her consciousness, identity, everything that makes her "her" - is extracted through a corrupted ritual and fed to Rangda, a manifestation of hunger and chaos. Her body doesn't die in the medical sense; her heart continues beating, lungs breathing. But "Skyler" no longer exists. What remains is a vessel.

### The Setup
- The hostel is run by a Balinese family who were corrupted generations ago
- They discovered that Rangda could be appeased by feeding her Western souls, sparing local communities
- The "soul cleansing" is actually a soul extraction, marketed to naive spiritual tourists
- Participants are primed through days of restricted eating, sleep deprivation, and "cleansing" herbs that open them psychically

### The Trigger
During the ceremony's climax, participants are asked to "open themselves completely to transformation." Skyler, having been prepared over several days, genuinely surrenders. This creates the opening the entity needs.

### The Process
1. Four days of "preparation" weaken Skyler's natural spiritual defenses
2. The ceremony begins with genuine-seeming meditation and chanting
3. Participants enter trance states enhanced by herbal smoke
4. The shaman marks Skyler as the chosen "vessel" (she thinks it's an honor)
5. The invocation shifts to something older, darker
6. Skyler feels a pulling sensation at her core
7. She sees something vast and hungry waiting in the darkness
8. Her soul is extracted, leaving her body behind
9. The body continues to function but there's no one inside

## Research Notes
- Balinese cosmology includes both protective and dangerous entities; Rangda represents chaos
- Traditional practices include trance states and spirit communication
- "Spiritual bypassing" is a documented psychological phenomenon
- The physiological state of a body without consciousness would resemble persistent vegetative state

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Skyler has completed four days of preparation. She's been fasting, drinking ceremonial teas, sleeping only four hours per night, and participating in "opening" rituals. She feels "lighter," "more connected" - not recognizing these as symptoms of depletion and suggestibility. Her phone has been surrendered "to focus on inner journey."

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
The final ceremony begins at dusk. Six participants in white. The shaman (Made Surya) marks Skyler's forehead with ash, calling her "the most open." She's positioned at the center. The chanting begins.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)
Write this sequence in full detail:

**Hour 0-2:**
Standard meditation deepening. Incense, drums, chanting in Balinese. Skyler feels herself drifting, pleasant dissociation. She's composing the Instagram caption in her head: "The moment everything changed..."

**Hour 2-3:**
The tone shifts. The chanting becomes less melodic, more rhythmic, insistent. Skyler can't open her eyes. She's not alarmed yet - this must be the deeper state. She feels herself becoming very still.

**Hour 3-4:**
Skyler becomes aware of a presence. Not the shaman. Something vast, ancient, feminine, hungry. She sees nothing but feels its attention on her like heat from a fire. She tries to withdraw but her body won't respond.

**Final Minutes:**
The extraction begins. It feels like being turned inside out. Skyler experiences every memory, every thought, every moment of her existence being pulled from her like thread from a spool. She sees her childhood, her mother, the day she changed her name, the real reason she couldn't stop running. For one instant, she finally understands herself completely. Then she's gone.

**Final Moment:**
Skyler's eyes open. Her body sits up. She breathes. But the person who looks out of those eyes is no one. Empty. The shaman thanks her for her "contribution to balance." Her body is led to a recovery room where it will be kept alive for weeks before the family arranges for her to be "found" at a scenic location with "natural causes."

### Immediate Aftermath
Her body is technically alive but non-responsive. Eventually "discovered" by hikers near a waterfall - apparent suicide or accident of spiritual tourist. Authorities are used to this. Her mother is notified. The hostel books more guests.

## Sensory Details

### Sight
A progression from darkness to visions of her life, to the face of Rangda - wild-haired, fanged, vast beyond comprehension, somehow beautiful and terrible simultaneously

### Sound
Chanting that becomes less like human voices and more like the sound of reality tearing, then absolute silence

### Touch/Pain
Not pain exactly - a pulling, an unraveling, like being slowly unwound from the inside. Then nothing at all.

### Smell
Incense, then something older - volcanic ash, ancient forest, something burning that might be her

### Taste
The bitter herbs she drank earlier, then nothing, a mouth that belongs to no one

## The Horror Element
The horror is twofold: first, the violation of having your very self consumed. Second, the realization that she had been so desperate for transformation that she literally surrendered herself to be destroyed. She got exactly what she asked for.

## The Comedy Element
Her final Instagram caption (composed mentally in the early ceremony) was going to be "The moment I became infinite." In a terrible way, she was right.

## Writing Notes

### Pacing the Death
Slow, hypnotic build through the ceremony, then sudden horrible acceleration when the extraction begins. Mirror the manipulation: seductive slowness that hides what's really happening.

### POV During Death
Stay in Skyler's head until the extraction, then briefly shift to external observer to show the horror of what's left behind.

### Graphic Content Level
- 6/10 - Psychological horror over physical; the body isn't damaged, which makes it worse
- Show the internal experience of losing yourself; imply what happens to the empty body
